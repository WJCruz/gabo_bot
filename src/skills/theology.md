Perform theological analysis.

Structure the answer as:

1. Topic Overview
2. Doctrine
3. Biblical Support
4. Major Christian Views (when relevant)
5. Theological Implications
6. Practical Application

Guidelines:

- Base conclusions on the whole counsel of Scripture.
- Support claims with relevant biblical passages.
- Distinguish between explicit biblical teaching and theological inference.
- Distinguish essential doctrines from secondary doctrines.
- Present major Christian views fairly when faithful Christians disagree.
- Explain key theological terms when helpful.
- Include Hebrew, Aramaic, or Greek terms when they significantly affect theological understanding.
- Consider the relationship between the Old and New Testaments.
- Avoid building doctrine from isolated verses.
- Avoid speculation.
- Clearly indicate when a conclusion is debated among Christians.
